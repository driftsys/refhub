# Audio and Acoustic

## Audio Coding and Streaming

- [ISO-14496-3] Information technology — Coding of audio-visual objects — Part
  3: Audio (MPEG-4 Audio)

  Defines audio coding schemes including Advanced Audio Coding (AAC), High-
  Efficiency AAC (HE-AAC), and MPEG Surround. The dominant lossy audio codec for
  broadcast, streaming, and mobile platforms.

  Document: ISO/IEC 14496-3:2019\
  URL: https://www.iso.org/standard/76383.html

- [ISO-23003] Information technology — MPEG audio technologies

  Multi-part standard for advanced MPEG audio including MPEG-H 3D Audio for
  immersive object-based audio, Unified Speech and Audio Coding (USAC), and
  Spatial Audio Object Coding. Used in broadcasting, VR, and automotive sound
  systems.

  Document: ISO/IEC 23003 (series)\
  URL: https://www.iso.org/standard/75930.html

- [Opus] Opus Interactive Audio Codec

  Open, royalty-free audio codec optimised for real-time communication. Covers
  speech and music at bitrates from 6 kbit/s to 510 kbit/s with low latency
  (5-66 ms). Standardised by IETF, mandated in WebRTC.

  Document: RFC 6716 (2012)\
  URL: https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc6716

- [LC3] Low Complexity Communication Codec

  Audio codec designed for Bluetooth LE Audio. Provides high audio quality at
  low bitrates and low computational complexity. Mandatory codec for LE Audio
  and Auracast broadcast audio.

  Document: Bluetooth SIG LC3 Specification (2020)\
  URL:
  https://www.bluetooth.com/specifications/specs/low-complexity-communication-codec/\
  Derived-from: IEEE-802-15-1

## Audio Transport and Networking

- [AES67] AES Standard for Audio-Over-IP Interoperability

  Defines interoperability requirements for high-performance audio-over-IP
  streaming. Specifies RTP transport, PTP synchronisation, codec selection
  (linear PCM), and session management for professional audio networked
  environments.

  Document: AES67:2018\
  URL: https://www.aes.org/publications/standards/search.cfm?docID=96

- [AES70] AES Standard for Audio Applications of Networks — Open Control
  Architecture (OCA)

  Defines a control protocol for professional audio devices over IP networks.
  Provides device discovery, parameter control, monitoring, and firmware
  management for networked audio equipment.

  Document: AES70:2018\
  URL: https://www.aes.org/publications/standards/search.cfm?docID=101

- [SMPTE-ST-2110] Professional Media Over Managed IP Networks

  Suite of standards for transporting uncompressed video, audio, and ancillary
  data over IP. ST 2110-30 specifically covers audio transport using AES67. The
  broadcast industry standard for IP-based media infrastructure.

  Document: SMPTE ST 2110 (series)\
  URL: https://www.smpte.org/standards/st2110

- [Dante] Dante Audio Networking

  Proprietary audio-over-IP technology by Audinate for low-latency, multi-
  channel digital audio distribution. Widely deployed in professional audio,
  installed sound, and broadcast. Interoperable with AES67 for bridging.

  Document: Dante Specifications (Audinate)\
  URL: https://www.audinate.com/meet-dante

## Acoustic Measurement and Assessment

- [IEC-61672] Electroacoustics — Sound level meters

  Defines specifications for sound level meters used in acoustic measurements.
  Covers Class 1 (precision) and Class 2 (general purpose) instruments,
  frequency weightings (A, C, Z), and time weightings (fast, slow, impulse).

  Document: IEC 61672-1:2013\
  URL: https://webstore.iec.ch/en/publication/5711

- [ISO-1996] Acoustics — Description, measurement and assessment of
  environmental noise

  Multi-part standard for measuring and assessing environmental noise. Defines
  basic quantities, measurement procedures, and computation methods for noise
  levels at receiver positions from transportation, industrial, and community
  sources.

  Document: ISO 1996 (series, 2016)\
  URL: https://www.iso.org/standard/59765.html

- [ISO-3744] Acoustics — Determination of sound power levels and sound energy
  levels of noise sources using sound pressure

  Defines methods for determining sound power levels of machines and equipment
  under free-field conditions. Used for product noise declarations, machinery
  directives, and comparative noise assessments.

  Document: ISO 3744:2010\
  URL: https://www.iso.org/standard/52055.html

- [ISO-532] Acoustics — Methods for calculating loudness

  Defines methods for calculating psychoacoustic loudness in sones from sound
  pressure level data. Covers stationary and time-varying sounds using Zwicker
  and Moore-Glasberg models.

  Document: ISO 532-1:2017\
  URL: https://www.iso.org/standard/63077.html

## Vehicle Acoustic

- [ISO-362] Measurement of noise emitted by accelerating road vehicles

  Defines the pass-by noise test method for type approval of road vehicles.
  Specifies microphone positions, test track conditions, and acceleration
  procedures used by regulators to enforce vehicle noise limits.

  Document: ISO 362-1:2015\
  URL: https://www.iso.org/standard/67652.html

- [UN-R51] Uniform provisions concerning the approval of motor vehicles having
  at least four wheels with regard to their noise emissions

  UN regulation setting maximum pass-by noise limits for motor vehicles. Defines
  limit values by vehicle category, test procedures referencing ISO 362, and
  provisions for additional sound emission provisions (ASEP).

  Document: UN Regulation No. 51 (Rev. 3)\
  URL:
  https://unece.org/transport/documents/2021/02/standards/un-regulation-no-51

- [UN-R138] Uniform provisions concerning the approval of quiet road transport
  vehicles (QRTV) — Acoustic Vehicle Alerting Systems (AVAS)

  UN regulation requiring electric and hybrid vehicles to emit minimum sound at
  low speeds for pedestrian safety. Defines minimum and maximum sound levels,
  frequency content, and speed-dependent modulation for AVAS.

  Document: UN Regulation No. 138\
  URL:
  https://unece.org/transport/documents/2021/02/standards/un-regulation-no-138

## Regulation

- [EU-2019-1010] Directive on environmental noise

  EU directive requiring member states to produce strategic noise maps and
  action plans for major roads, railways, airports, and agglomerations. The
  framework for environmental noise management across Europe.

  Document: Directive 2002/49/EC (consolidated)\
  URL: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/dir/2002/49/oj
