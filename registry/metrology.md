# Metrology

Standards and publications for measurement science covering fundamental
concepts, measurement uncertainty, SI units, calibration, traceability, and
legal metrology. Provides the metrological foundation referenced by testing and
calibration laboratories worldwide.

**Contents:** [Standard](#standard) | [Publication](#publication)

## Standard

- [ISO-10012] Measurement management systems — Requirements for measurement
  processes and measuring equipment

  Defines requirements for measurement management systems ensuring metrological
  confirmation of measuring equipment and validation of measurement processes.
  Used by organisations requiring demonstrable measurement capability beyond ISO
  9001 requirements.

  Document: ISO 10012:2003\
  URL: <https://www.iso.org/standard/26033.html>\
  Label: Standard\
  Keywords: measurement management, metrological confirmation, measuring
  equipment, measurement process validation, quality management

- [ISO-13528] Statistical methods for use in proficiency testing by
  interlaboratory comparison

  Defines statistical methods for evaluating laboratory performance through
  proficiency testing schemes. Specifies z-scores, robust statistics, and
  assigned value determination for comparing measurement results across
  participating laboratories.

  Document: ISO 13528:2022\
  URL: <https://www.iso.org/standard/78879.html>\
  Label: Standard\
  Keywords: proficiency testing, interlaboratory comparison, z-score, robust
  statistics, laboratory performance, assigned value

- [ISO-17025] General requirements for the competence of testing and calibration
  laboratories

  Requirements for laboratories to demonstrate competence, impartiality, and
  consistent operation. Covers method validation, measurement uncertainty,
  equipment calibration, and quality assurance of test results. See quality
  domain for full entry.

  Document: ISO/IEC 17025:2017\
  URL: <https://www.iso.org/standard/66912.html>\
  Label: Standard\
  Keywords: laboratory accreditation, testing, calibration, method validation,
  measurement uncertainty, quality assurance, competence

- [ISO-5725] Accuracy (trueness and precision) of measurement methods and
  results

  Multi-part standard defining methods for determining accuracy of measurement
  methods. Covers repeatability, reproducibility, trueness, intermediate
  precision, and alternative methods for assessing measurement method
  performance.

  Document: ISO 5725 (series, 2023)\
  URL: <https://www.iso.org/standard/69418.html>\
  Label: Standard\
  Keywords: accuracy, trueness, precision, repeatability, reproducibility,
  measurement method, interlaboratory study

- [OIML-R76] Non-automatic weighing instruments

  International recommendation defining metrological and technical requirements
  for non-automatic weighing instruments. Specifies accuracy classes (I-IIII),
  maximum permissible errors, and type approval test procedures for scales used
  in trade, industry, and healthcare.

  Document: OIML R 76-1 (2006)\
  URL:
  <https://www.oiml.org/en/publications/recommendations/publication_view?p_type=1&p_status=1>\
  Label: Standard\
  Keywords: weighing instruments, scales, accuracy class, maximum permissible
  error, type approval, legal metrology, OIML

## Publication

- [GUM] Guide to the Expression of Uncertainty in Measurement

  Defines the internationally accepted framework for evaluating and expressing
  measurement uncertainty. Specifies Type A (statistical) and Type B (other)
  evaluation methods, combined uncertainty, expanded uncertainty, and coverage
  factors. The basis for all uncertainty budgets in accredited laboratories.

  Document: JCGM 100:2008 (GUM)\
  URL: <https://www.bipm.org/en/committees/jc/jcgm/publications>\
  Label: Publication\
  Keywords: measurement uncertainty, Type A evaluation, Type B evaluation,
  combined uncertainty, expanded uncertainty, coverage factor, uncertainty
  budget

- [ILAC-P10] ILAC Policy on Metrological Traceability of Measurement Results

  ILAC policy defining requirements for metrological traceability in accredited
  laboratories. Specifies that measurement results must be traceable to SI units
  through an unbroken chain of calibrations with stated uncertainties.

  Document: ILAC P10:2020\
  URL: <https://ilac.org/publications-and-resources/ilac-policy-series/>\
  Label: Publication\
  Keywords: metrological traceability, calibration chain, SI units, accredited
  laboratory, ILAC, traceability policy, measurement results

- [OIML-D1] Considerations for a Law on Metrology

  OIML document providing model legislation for national metrology laws. Covers
  legal units of measurement, metrological control, type approval, verification,
  and market surveillance of measuring instruments used in regulated sectors.

  Document: OIML D 1 (2020)\
  URL: <https://www.oiml.org/en/publications/documents>\
  Label: Publication\
  Keywords: legal metrology, model legislation, metrological control, type
  approval, verification, market surveillance, OIML

- [SI] The International System of Units

  Defines the seven SI base units (metre, kilogram, second, ampere, kelvin,
  mole, candela) and their 2019 redefinitions based on fixed fundamental
  constants. The universal measurement system mandated by international trade
  and referenced by all technical standards.

  Document: SI Brochure (9th Edition, 2019)\
  URL: <https://www.bipm.org/en/publications/si-brochure>\
  Label: Publication\
  Keywords: SI units, metre, kilogram, second, ampere, kelvin, mole, candela,
  fundamental constants, BIPM

- [VIM] International Vocabulary of Metrology — Basic and General Concepts and
  Associated Terms

  Defines the fundamental vocabulary of metrology: measurement, measurand,
  traceability, uncertainty, accuracy, precision, calibration, and verification.
  The normative terminology reference for all measurement- related standards and
  regulations.

  Document: JCGM 200:2012 (VIM 3rd Edition)\
  URL: <https://www.bipm.org/en/committees/jc/jcgm/publications>\
  Label: Publication\
  Keywords: metrology vocabulary, measurand, traceability, uncertainty,
  accuracy, precision, calibration, verification, JCGM
