# Acoustic

## Measurement and Assessment

- [IEC-61672] Electroacoustics — Sound level meters

  Defines specifications for sound level meters used in acoustic measurements.
  Covers Class 1 (precision) and Class 2 (general purpose) instruments,
  frequency weightings (A, C, Z), and time weightings (fast, slow, impulse).

  Document: IEC 61672-1:2013\
  URL: https://webstore.iec.ch/en/publication/5711

- [ISO-1996] Acoustics — Description, measurement and assessment of
  environmental noise

  Multi-part standard for measuring and assessing environmental noise. Defines
  basic quantities, measurement procedures, and computation methods for noise
  levels at receiver positions from transportation, industrial, and community
  sources.

  Document: ISO 1996 (series, 2016)\
  URL: https://www.iso.org/standard/59765.html

- [ISO-3744] Acoustics — Determination of sound power levels and sound energy
  levels of noise sources using sound pressure

  Defines methods for determining sound power levels of machines and equipment
  under free-field conditions. Used for product noise declarations, machinery
  directives, and comparative noise assessments.

  Document: ISO 3744:2010\
  URL: https://www.iso.org/standard/52055.html

- [ISO-532] Acoustics — Methods for calculating loudness

  Defines methods for calculating psychoacoustic loudness in sones from sound
  pressure level data. Covers stationary and time-varying sounds using Zwicker
  and Moore-Glasberg models.

  Document: ISO 532-1:2017\
  URL: https://www.iso.org/standard/63077.html

- [IEC-61260] Electroacoustics — Octave-band and fractional-octave-band filters

  Defines performance requirements for analogue and digital octave-band and
  fractional-octave-band filters used in acoustic measurements. Specifies filter
  characteristics, tolerances, and test methods for Class 0, 1, and 2
  instruments.

  Document: IEC 61260-1:2014\
  URL: https://webstore.iec.ch/en/publication/5066

- [ISO-3745] Acoustics — Determination of sound power levels and sound energy
  levels of noise sources using sound pressure — Precision methods for anechoic
  rooms and hemi-anechoic rooms

  Defines precision methods for sound power determination in anechoic and
  hemi-anechoic rooms. The most accurate method for product noise
  characterisation, used for reference measurements and type testing.

  Document: ISO 3745:2012\
  URL: https://www.iso.org/standard/45362.html

## Vibration

- [ISO-2631] Mechanical vibration and shock — Evaluation of human exposure to
  whole-body vibration

  Defines methods for evaluating human exposure to whole-body vibration in
  vehicles, buildings, and workplaces. Specifies frequency weightings, axes, and
  exposure limits for health, comfort, and motion sickness.

  Document: ISO 2631-1:1997\
  URL: https://www.iso.org/standard/7612.html

- [ISO-5349] Mechanical vibration — Measurement and evaluation of human exposure
  to hand-transmitted vibration

  Defines methods for measuring and evaluating hand-arm vibration from powered
  tools and machinery. Specifies frequency weightings and daily exposure limits
  to prevent hand-arm vibration syndrome (HAVS).

  Document: ISO 5349-1:2001\
  URL: https://www.iso.org/standard/32355.html

## Vehicle Acoustic

- [ISO-362] Measurement of noise emitted by accelerating road vehicles

  Defines the pass-by noise test method for type approval of road vehicles.
  Specifies microphone positions, test track conditions, and acceleration
  procedures used by regulators to enforce vehicle noise limits.

  Document: ISO 362-1:2015\
  URL: https://www.iso.org/standard/67652.html

- [ISO-5128] Acoustics — Measurement of noise inside motor vehicles

  Defines methods for measuring interior noise in motor vehicles under
  stationary and moving conditions. Used for NVH (noise, vibration, harshness)
  assessment, benchmarking, and interior sound quality evaluation.

  Document: ISO 5128:1980\
  URL: https://www.iso.org/standard/11126.html

- [UN-R51] Uniform provisions concerning the approval of motor vehicles having
  at least four wheels with regard to their noise emissions

  UN regulation setting maximum pass-by noise limits for motor vehicles. Defines
  limit values by vehicle category, test procedures referencing ISO 362, and
  provisions for additional sound emission provisions (ASEP).

  Document: UN Regulation No. 51 (Rev. 3)\
  URL:
  https://unece.org/transport/documents/2021/02/standards/un-regulation-no-51

- [UN-R138] Uniform provisions concerning the approval of quiet road transport
  vehicles (QRTV) — Acoustic Vehicle Alerting Systems (AVAS)

  UN regulation requiring electric and hybrid vehicles to emit minimum sound at
  low speeds for pedestrian safety. Defines minimum and maximum sound levels,
  frequency content, and speed-dependent modulation for AVAS.

  Document: UN Regulation No. 138\
  URL:
  https://unece.org/transport/documents/2021/02/standards/un-regulation-no-138

## Regulation

- [EU-2002-49] Directive relating to the assessment and management of
  environmental noise

  EU directive requiring member states to produce strategic noise maps and
  action plans for major roads, railways, airports, and agglomerations. The
  framework for environmental noise management across Europe.

  Document: Directive 2002/49/EC (consolidated)\
  URL: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/dir/2002/49/oj
