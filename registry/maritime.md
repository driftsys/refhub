# Maritime

## Safety

- [SOLAS] International Convention for the Safety of Life at Sea

  The most important international treaty governing maritime safety. Defines
  minimum standards for ship construction, equipment, and operation including
  fire protection, life-saving appliances, radio communication, navigation, and
  cargo handling.

  Document: SOLAS 1974 (consolidated 2024)\
  URL:
  https://www.imo.org/en/About/Conventions/Pages/International-Convention-for-the-Safety-of-Life-at-Sea-(SOLAS),-1974.aspx

- [ISM-Code] International Safety Management Code

  IMO code establishing an international standard for the safe management and
  operation of ships and pollution prevention. Requires shipping companies to
  implement safety management systems (SMS) with documented procedures and audit
  processes.

  Document: IMO Resolution A.741(18) (amended)\
  URL: https://www.imo.org/en/OurWork/HumanElement/Pages/ISMCode.aspx

- [MARPOL] International Convention for the Prevention of Pollution from Ships

  IMO convention covering prevention of pollution from ships by oil, noxious
  liquid substances, harmful substances in packaged form, sewage, garbage, and
  air emissions. Defines emission control areas and fuel sulphur limits.

  Document: MARPOL 73/78 (consolidated)\
  URL:
  https://www.imo.org/en/About/Conventions/Pages/International-Convention-for-the-Prevention-of-Pollution-from-Ships-(MARPOL).aspx

## Navigation and Communication

- [IEC-61162] Maritime navigation and radiocommunication equipment and systems —
  Digital interfaces

  Defines digital interface standards for maritime navigation equipment. Covers
  NMEA 0183 serial (-1), high-speed interfaces (-2), IEC 61162-450 Ethernet
  (-450), and lightweight Ethernet (-460) for interconnecting bridge equipment.

  Document: IEC 61162 (series)\
  URL: https://webstore.iec.ch/en/publication/4713

- [IEC-61174] Maritime navigation and radiocommunication equipment and systems —
  Electronic chart display and information system (ECDIS)

  Defines performance standards for ECDIS used as the primary means of
  navigation. Specifies chart display, route planning, route monitoring, and
  alarm functionality replacing paper nautical charts.

  Document: IEC 61174:2015\
  URL: https://webstore.iec.ch/en/publication/4731

- [VDES] VHF Data Exchange System

  ITU-defined system for maritime digital communication replacing AIS
  message-based data exchange. Provides ship-to-ship, ship-to-shore, and
  satellite links with higher bandwidth for e-navigation services and maritime
  safety information.

  Document: ITU-R M.2092-1 (2022)\
  URL: https://www.itu.int/rec/R-REC-M.2092

## Classification and Certification

- [IACS-UR] International Association of Classification Societies — Unified
  Requirements

  Unified technical requirements adopted by major classification societies (DNV,
  Lloyd's, Bureau Veritas, ABS, etc.). Cover structural design, hull surveys,
  machinery, electrical systems, and materials for seagoing ships.

  Document: IACS UR (various)\
  URL: https://iacs.org.uk/resolutions/unified-requirements

- [DNV-Rules] DNV Rules for Classification of Ships

  Comprehensive classification rules defining technical standards for ship
  design, construction, and operation. Cover hull structure, machinery,
  electrical systems, automation, and special vessel types. Basis for statutory
  certification under SOLAS.

  Document: DNV Rules for Classification (annual)\
  URL: https://www.dnv.com/rules-standards/

## Autonomous Vessels

- [IMO-MASS] Maritime Autonomous Surface Ships

  IMO regulatory framework for maritime autonomous surface ships. Defines four
  degrees of autonomy from crewed ships with automated processes to fully
  autonomous vessels. Establishes interim guidelines for MASS trials and
  operations.

  Document: IMO MSC.1/Circ.1638 (2021) / MASS Code (in development)\
  URL:
  https://www.imo.org/en/MediaCentre/HotTopics/Pages/Autonomous-shipping.aspx

## Cybersecurity

- [IACS-UR-E26] Cyber resilience of ships

  IACS unified requirement for cyber resilience of ships entering into service
  from July 2024. Defines cybersecurity requirements for computer- based systems
  onboard covering network architecture, access control, malware protection, and
  security event monitoring.

  Document: IACS UR E26 (2022)\
  URL: https://iacs.org.uk/resolutions/unified-requirements
