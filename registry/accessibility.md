# Accessibility

Standards, regulations, and guidelines governing the design and delivery of
products, services, and digital content that are usable by people with
disabilities. Covers web, software, hardware, and document accessibility.

**Contents:** [Standard](#standard) | [Regulation](#regulation)

## Standard

- [ATAG] Authoring Tool Accessibility Guidelines

  W3C standard defining how authoring tools (CMS, WYSIWYG editors, code editors)
  should support production of accessible content and be accessible to authors
  with disabilities themselves.

  Document: ATAG 2.0 (2015)\
  URL: <https://www.w3.org/TR/ATAG20/>\
  Label: Standard\
  Keywords: authoring tools, CMS, WYSIWYG, content creation, accessibility, W3C,
  assistive technology

- [EN-301-549] Accessibility requirements for ICT products and services

  European standard defining accessibility requirements for information and
  communication technology. Covers web, software, hardware, documentation, and
  support services. The harmonised standard under the EU Web Accessibility and
  Accessibility Act directives.

  Document: ETSI EN 301 549 V3.2.1 (2021)\
  URL:
  https://www.etsi.org/deliver/etsi_en/301500_301599/301549/03.02.01_60/en_301549v030201p.pdf\
  Label: Standard\
  Keywords: ICT accessibility, ETSI, European harmonised standard, web,
  software, hardware, documentation, support services

- [IEC-63008] Household and similar electrical appliances — Accessibility of
  control elements, doors and lids, and drawers

  Defines accessibility requirements for controls and physical elements of
  household appliances. Covers tactile identification, operability, reach
  requirements, and feedback for users with reduced dexterity, vision, or
  strength.

  Document: IEC 63008:2020\
  URL: <https://webstore.iec.ch/en/publication/29530>\
  Label: Standard\
  Keywords: household appliances, tactile identification, operability,
  dexterity, physical accessibility, controls, reach requirements

- [ISO-9241] Ergonomics of human-system interaction

  Multi-part standard covering usability and accessibility of interactive
  systems. Part 171 defines guidance on software accessibility, Part 20 covers
  accessibility of IT equipment and services, and Part 110 defines interaction
  principles.

  Document: ISO 9241 (series)\
  URL: <https://www.iso.org/standard/63785.html>\
  Label: Standard\
  Keywords: ergonomics, usability, human-system interaction, software
  accessibility, interaction principles, IT equipment

- [PDF-UA] PDF/Universal Accessibility

  ISO standard defining requirements for accessible PDF documents. Specifies
  tagged PDF structure, alternative text, reading order, and navigation
  requirements enabling assistive technology access to PDF content.

  Document: ISO 14289-2:2024 (PDF/UA-2)\
  URL: <https://www.iso.org/standard/82278.html>\
  Label: Standard\
  Keywords: PDF, document accessibility, tagged PDF, alternative text, reading
  order, navigation, assistive technology

- [WAI-ARIA] Accessible Rich Internet Applications

  W3C specification defining semantics for making dynamic web content and custom
  UI components accessible to assistive technologies. Provides roles, states,
  and properties that supplement HTML for complex widgets, live regions, and
  application-like interactions.

  Document: WAI-ARIA 1.2 (2023)\
  URL: <https://www.w3.org/TR/wai-aria-1.2/>\
  Label: Standard\
  Keywords: WAI-ARIA, web accessibility, assistive technology, roles, states,
  properties, dynamic content, widgets, W3C

- [WCAG] Web Content Accessibility Guidelines

  W3C standard defining how to make web content more accessible to people with
  disabilities. Organised around four principles — perceivable, operable,
  understandable, robust — with three conformance levels (A, AA, AAA).
  Referenced by most accessibility regulations worldwide.

  Document: WCAG 2.2 (2023)\
  URL: <https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG22/>\
  Label: Standard\
  Keywords: web accessibility, WCAG, perceivable, operable, understandable,
  robust, conformance levels, W3C, disabilities

## Regulation

- [ADA] Americans with Disabilities Act

  U.S. federal civil rights law prohibiting discrimination against individuals
  with disabilities. Title III covers public accommodations and commercial
  facilities, increasingly applied to websites and digital services through DOJ
  guidance and case law.

  Document: 42 U.S.C. ch. 126 (1990)\
  URL: <https://www.ada.gov/>\
  Label: Regulation\
  Keywords: ADA, civil rights, disability discrimination, public accommodations,
  digital accessibility, United States, federal law

- [EU-Accessibility-Act] European Accessibility Act

  EU directive establishing common accessibility requirements for products and
  services including computers, smartphones, ATMs, ticketing machines, e-books,
  e-commerce, and banking. Mandatory from June 2025.

  Document: Directive (EU) 2019/882\
  URL: <https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/dir/2019/882/oj>\
  Label: Regulation\
  Keywords: European Accessibility Act, EU directive, products, services,
  smartphones, ATMs, e-commerce, banking, e-books

- [EU-WAD] Web Accessibility Directive

  EU directive requiring public sector websites and mobile applications to meet
  accessibility standards. Mandates conformance with EN 301 549 (which
  references WCAG 2.1 Level AA) and requires accessibility statements and
  feedback mechanisms.

  Document: Directive (EU) 2016/2102\
  URL: <https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/dir/2016/2102/oj>\
  Label: Regulation\
  Keywords: Web Accessibility Directive, EU directive, public sector, websites,
  mobile applications, WCAG, accessibility statements

- [Section-508] Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act

  U.S. federal requirement mandating that electronic and information technology
  developed, procured, or used by federal agencies be accessible to people with
  disabilities. References WCAG 2.0 Level AA since the 2018 refresh.

  Document: 36 CFR Part 1194 (2017 refresh)\
  URL: <https://www.section508.gov/>\
  Label: Regulation\
  Keywords: Section 508, Rehabilitation Act, federal agencies, electronic
  information technology, WCAG, procurement, United States
