# Electromagnetic Compatibility

## Generic Standards

- [IEC-61000-1] Electromagnetic compatibility (EMC) — General

  Foundational EMC standard defining terms, phenomena, and general
  considerations for electromagnetic compatibility. Establishes the framework
  referenced by all product-specific and environment-specific EMC standards.

  Document: IEC 61000-1 (series)\
  URL: <https://webstore.iec.ch/en/publication/4125>

- [IEC-61000-4] Electromagnetic compatibility (EMC) — Testing and measurement
  techniques

  Defines EMC test methods including electrostatic discharge (ESD, -4-2),
  radiated immunity (-4-3), electrical fast transient/burst (-4-4), surge
  (-4-5), conducted immunity (-4-6), power frequency magnetic field (-4-8), and
  voltage dips/interruptions (-4-11).

  Document: IEC 61000-4 (series)\
  URL: <https://webstore.iec.ch/en/publication/4189>

- [IEC-61000-6] Electromagnetic compatibility (EMC) — Generic standards

  Generic emission and immunity standards for residential, commercial,
  light-industrial (-6-1/-6-3) and industrial (-6-2/-6-4) environments. Applied
  when no product-specific EMC standard exists.

  Document: IEC 61000-6 (series)\
  URL: <https://webstore.iec.ch/en/publication/4243>

- [CISPR-11] Industrial, scientific and medical equipment — Radio-frequency
  disturbance characteristics

  Emission limits and measurement methods for ISM equipment. Defines Group 1 and
  Group 2 equipment classes with conducted and radiated emission limits for
  laboratory and in-situ testing.

  Document: CISPR 11:2024\
  URL: <https://webstore.iec.ch/en/publication/68982>

- [CISPR-32] Electromagnetic compatibility of multimedia equipment — Emission
  requirements

  Emission requirements for multimedia equipment including IT, audio/video, and
  broadcast receiver equipment. Replaces CISPR 13 and CISPR 22. Defines Class A
  (commercial) and Class B (residential) limits.

  Document: CISPR 32:2015+AMD1:2019\
  URL: <https://webstore.iec.ch/en/publication/65007>

- [CISPR-35] Electromagnetic compatibility of multimedia equipment — Immunity
  requirements

  Immunity requirements for multimedia equipment. Companion to CISPR 32 covering
  immunity to ESD, radiated fields, EFT/burst, surge, and conducted disturbances
  for IT and multimedia products.

  Document: CISPR 35:2016\
  URL: <https://webstore.iec.ch/en/publication/24657>

## Automotive EMC

- [ISO-11452] Road vehicles — Component test methods for electrical disturbances
  from narrowband radiated electromagnetic energy

  Defines test methods for evaluating immunity of vehicle components to radiated
  electromagnetic disturbances. Covers absorber-lined chamber, TEM cell, bulk
  current injection, stripline, and direct power injection methods.

  Document: ISO 11452 (series, 2020)\
  URL: <https://www.iso.org/standard/68559.html>

- [CISPR-25] Vehicles, boats and internal combustion engines — Radio disturbance
  characteristics

  Emission limits and measurement methods for components and modules in vehicles
  and boats. Defines conducted and radiated emission limits for protection of
  on-board receivers (AM, FM, TV, GNSS, cellular).

  Document: CISPR 25:2021\
  URL: <https://webstore.iec.ch/en/publication/64551>

- [ISO-11451] Road vehicles — Vehicle test methods for electrical disturbances
  from narrowband radiated electromagnetic energy

  Defines whole-vehicle EMC immunity test methods. Covers absorber-lined
  shielded enclosure (ALSE) testing, free-field testing, and current injection
  methods at the vehicle level.

  Document: ISO 11451 (series, 2015)\
  URL: <https://www.iso.org/standard/59609.html>

## Regulation

- [EU-EMC-Directive] Directive on the harmonisation of the laws relating to
  electromagnetic compatibility

  EU directive establishing essential requirements for EMC of electrical and
  electronic equipment placed on the EU market. Requires equipment to neither
  cause excessive electromagnetic disturbance nor be unduly affected by it.

  Document: Directive 2014/30/EU\
  URL: <https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/dir/2014/30/oj>

- [FCC-Part-15] FCC Rules Part 15 — Radio Frequency Devices

  U.S. federal regulation governing emissions from intentional and unintentional
  radiators. Defines Class A (commercial) and Class B (residential) emission
  limits for digital devices and unlicensed radio equipment.

  Document: 47 CFR Part 15\
  URL: <https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-47/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-15>

- [FCC-Part-18] FCC Rules Part 18 — Industrial, Scientific, and Medical
  Equipment

  U.S. federal regulation governing emissions from ISM equipment operating on
  designated ISM frequencies. Covers medical diathermy, industrial heating, and
  RF lighting equipment.

  Document: 47 CFR Part 18\
  URL: <https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-47/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-18>
